  I am an assistant professor in the Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern University. I received my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Washington, advised by Zoran Popović. My current research focuses on using video games to solve difficult scientific problems; I have delivered multiple TED talks on the topic. I am co-creator of the scientific discovery games Foldit and Nanocrafter and early math educational games including Refraction and Treefrog Treasure. I have also done research in real-time animation for games. I have previously worked at the Center for Game Science (as creative director), Square Enix, Electronic Arts, Pixar Animation Studios and the UC Berkeley Space Sciences Laboratory (on BOINC, the Berkeley Open Infrastructure for Network Computing).

If you are a student interested in working in game science research, feel free to contact me!
