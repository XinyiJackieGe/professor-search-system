Hi, I'm Brian!

I am an Assistant Professor at Columbia University's Dept. of Computer Science pursuing research in human–computer interaction (HCI). My students and I develop computers that can grant people new abilities, abilities that help people better experience the world. Our research is interdisciplinary and incorporates AI, sensing, vision, design (including game design), and accessibility.

→ I am actively recruiting Ph.D. students who are creative, persevering, and eager to take on hard technical problems. If you are interested in working with me, please review the Ph.D. program Web site and e-mail me with your interests and CV. Please also contact me if you wish to apply after the deadline. I expressly welcome underrepresented voices to HCI research.

I am also a research scientist at Snap Research, where I develop new concepts in human–computer interaction (HCI), social computing, games, and augmented reality. I am a member of Snap's HCI Research team and work closely with SnapLab, Snap's hardware division and the team behind Spectacles.
