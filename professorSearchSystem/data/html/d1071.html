Boyuan Chen

bchen@cs.columbia.edu

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Columbia University where I work on Computer Vision, Robotics, and Machine Learning. I am a member of Creative Machines Lab, advised by Professor Hod Lipson. I also work closely with Professor Carl Vondrick.

Previously, I also spent my time at Columbia Robotics Lab with Professor Peter Allen, Control System Group at the University of Manchester where I completed my undergraduate thesis, and Jilin University where I obtained my Bachelor degree from Biomedical Engineering and Electrical Engineering.

Research

My research is at the intersection of Computer Vision, Robotics, and Machine Learning. I am interested in endow machines the ability to acquire knowledge of the world through observation and interaction with the environment and other agents. Will it be possible to have machines to form their own understanding of the world, make decisions based on that, and keep improving themselves? How can we leverage large amount of data in the open world to build better learning systems from these raw signals such as videos, audios, and speech through self-suerpvision? Paths along the way of trying to answer these questions can lead to other interesting ideas about Artificial Intelligence in general.
