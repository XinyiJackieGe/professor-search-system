SIGGRAPH Asia 09 fast forward

What up yo! This is Hao, I’m a German born punky of Taiwanese descent doing computer graphics. I know, #epicfail. But I’m also an Associate Professor (with tenure), fashion mödel, and zombie at the Computer Science Department of the University of Southern California (USC) in Los Angeles, CEO of Pinscreen, a well-funded startup bringing AR to mobile communication, as well as the director of the Vision and Graphics Lab of the USC Institute for Creative Technologies. Here is our USC website and my official song! Before my faculty appointment, I was a research lead at Industrial Light & Magic (ILM) where I developed the next generation real-time performance capture technology for visual effects. Prior to joining the force, I spent an exciting year as a postdoc at Columbia and Princeton Universities. I worked on geometric capture of human performances with Eitan Grinspun and Szymon Rusinkiewicz who helped me bridge the gap between data capture and physical simulation. Right after obtaining my PhD from ETH Zurich, I spent a few months at EPFL, visiting my PhD advisor Mark Pauly. During grad school, I spent a wonderful summer at ILM and another one at Stanford University visiting Leo Guibas. I got my Master’s degree in CS from the University of Karlsruhe in Germany and went to a French high school in a boonie town called Saarbrücken.


My algorithms on dynamic shape reconstruction and non-rigid registration are widely deployed in the industry, ranging from leading visual effects studios to defense projects and manufacturers of state-of-the-art radiation therapy systems. My work on depth sensor-driven facial animation has also led to the Animoji feature on Apple’s iPhone X. Hey, some of my stuff even works in industry! I received the Office of Naval Research (ONR) Young Investigator Award in 2018, Google Faculty Research Award, the Okawa Foundation Research Grant, and the Andrew and Erna Viterbi Early Career Chair in 2015. I’ve been named one of the world’s top 35 innovator under 35 by MIT Technology review in 2013 and NextGen 10: Innovators under 40 by C-Suite Quaterly in 2014. I am ranked #1 in 2016, on the Top 10 Leaderboard of Computer Graphics research for the past five years by Microsoft Academic. In 2019, I have been named to the DARPA ISAT Study Group. My research is also featured in the March 2014 issue of Wired magazine and in MIT Technology Review in 2019. Here are some tips on how to write SIGGRAPH papers. I was awarded the SNF fellowship for prospective researchers in 2011 and Best Paper Award at SCA 2009. Our 3D hair scanning technology is deployed at Weta Digital, where I also worked on the 3D facial tracking for animating the digital Paul Walker in Furious 7. Oh! That gives me an Erdös-Bacon number of 5. In collaboration with Artec Group, we developed Shapify.me, a free software that allows anyone to create their own 3D miniature from home. I’m also the creator of the dynamic geometry processing tool, BeNTO3D, and the original co-author of the Kinect-based facial performance capture software, faceshift (acquired by Apple). Together with Oculus & Facebook, we prototyped the world’s first facial performance sensing virtual reality HMD to enable social interactions in cyberspace. Here is my interview on this technology from Oculus Connect 2. My work has been selected for SIGGRAPH Real-Time Live! for five times in a row (SIGGRAPH 2017, SIGGRAPH 2018, SIGGRAPH Asia 2018, SIGGRAPH 2019, and SIGGRAPH Asia 2019). The show celebrates the top jury-reviewed interactive novelties of the year. I’m member of the global future councils and co-curator for transformation maps at the World Economic Forum (WEF) and member of ACM SIGGRAPH, the Eurographics Association, the National Academy of Inventors, and the World Future Society (WFS). I have absolutely no clue why that even matters, but who cares? Oh, I’m also sometimes on TV shows (Buzzfeed’s Follow This, CBC’s The Fifth Estate, Takeshi Kitano’s Sekai Marumie TV) in the News (BBC, CNN,  Bloomberg, Fox News, CNBC, Spectrum News, Fox 11 News, PBS Newshour, CNET, CBS News, ABC News, i24NEWS, AP News, CNN, CCTV, Das Erste ARD, ZDF Heute, Canal+, Nippon TV, ABC Australia, Al Jazeera), on radio (Science Friday) in the NY Times, the LA Times (2015, 2018), the Washington Post, Scientific American, MIT Technology Review, The Verge, PC Mag, Neue Zürcher Zeitung, and fxguide!
