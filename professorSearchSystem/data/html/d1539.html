Boon Thau Loo is the RCA Professor of Artificial Intelligence in the Computer and Information Science (primary) and the Electrical and Systems Engineering (secondary) departments. As Penn Engineering Associate Dean, he oversees all engineering masterâ€™s and professional programs. He received his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the University of California at Berkeley in 2006. He received his M.S. degree from Stanford University in 2000, and his B.S. degree with highest honors from University of California-Berkeley in 1999. His research focuses on distributed data management systems, Internet-scale query processing, and the application of data-centric techniques and formal methods to the design, analysis and implementation of networked systems. In addition to his academic work, he actively participates in entrepreneurial activities involving technology transfer. He co-founded Gencore Systems (Netsil) in 2014, a cloud performance analytics company that spun out of his research team. The company was successfully acquired by Nutanix Inc in 2018.
